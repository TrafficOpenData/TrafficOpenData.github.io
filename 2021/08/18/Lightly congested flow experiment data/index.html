Introduction

 The experiment was carried out on Nov. 9, 2019, in the test field affiliated to Research Institute of Highway, Ministry of Transport, China. For a snapshot of the experiment, see Figure 1. In the experiment, 40 cars/drivers were recruited and classified into 8 groups A ~ H. The order of cars/drivers in each group is always fixed. The number of groups and their order change in different runs. Car/Driver No. is the unique identification for each car in all experiment Runs. Cars in group A is numbered as 1 ~ 5, group B as 6 ~ 10, …, group H as 36 ~ 40. The instruction to the drivers is “Drive as if you were on rush hour expressways. Follow the vehicle ahead closely, whenever safety permits”. High-precision GPS devices were installed on all of the vehicles to record their locations and velocities every 0.1 second.

Fig. 1. (a) A snapshot of the experiment. Due to the large circumference, the camera is not able to cover the whole track. (b) The trajectories of cars measured by the GPS installed on the cars.

 Initially the cars are stopped bumper-to-bumper. When a Run starts, the first car accelerates to ~20 km/h and travels at that speed until the last car starts up. Then the first car moves to catch up with the last car. Details of the experiment Runs are shown in Table 1 in chronological order.

Table 1 Details of the experiment Runs in chronological order.
* One vehicle has Electronic Stability Program (ESP) fault and leaves the track at t = 530 s.
** The same vehicle has ESP fault again and leaves the track at t =240 s.
*** GPS signal of one vehicle is missing.

##MAT data description

 The experimental data are exported to “.mat” file. The meaning of arrays in the “.mat” file is shown in Table 2. Note that the column in each array has been arranged in the sequence of vehicles for each run.

Table 2. The meaning of each array.

 The measurement errors of the GPS devices were within ± 1 m for location and within ± 1 km/h for velocity. The data sampling frequency is 10 Hz. Blank data are the missing GPS data due to the GPS device signal problem or vehicle breakdown.

*Please note that there are only part of the data in the current dataset shared with the public. We apologize for any inconvenience to you.
